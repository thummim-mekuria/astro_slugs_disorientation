History of Santa Cruz, CA

The original peoples of Santa Cruz County are the Awaswas nation of the Ohlone people, and Amuh Mutsen peoples. There are some vestiges of these peoples in the area today: the names of three nearby towns (Aptos, Soquel, and Zayante) are some of the only surviving names from the Awaswas language. At the time of colonization, this area was called Aulinta.

In 1848, following the Mexican-American war, the area was ceded to the United States. California, and Santa Cruz County, were established in 1850. The establishment of railroad lines in Santa Cruz in 1875-76, with the Santa Cruz & Felton Railroad and the Santa Cruz Railroad, provided market access for the city's timber, leather and limestone industries, thereby building a local economy maintained to this day.
